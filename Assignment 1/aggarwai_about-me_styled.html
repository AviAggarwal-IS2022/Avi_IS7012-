Statement of Purpose

I was 16 years old when the acclaimed TV series ‘Person of Interest’ engrossed me in the idea of training computers to analyze unfathomable amounts of data and return credible information. It was my introduction to the usage of Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence. The fascinating interaction between computer code and hardware drove me to pursue a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science Engineering.
During my Junior year, I interned with Octro Inc., where I cultivated an interest in Business Intelligence and Data Analytics. As an Intern, I increasingly used Tableau to plug in user data collected to prepare dashboards on gameplay and purchase patterns across demographics and geographies, whilst identifying potential in-app purchases, retention incentives, return user analysis, and casual player behavioral analytics. The insights acquired were shared across business development teams, aiding in customer retention and marketing decisions.
    Then, at Counterpoint Technologies Market Research as an Associate, I worked on developing the company's flagship data analytics project - Digital Dashboards, where I built Tableau Dashboards for subscribing clients using the company’s core Smartphone Market Performance and Intelligence databases. I also worked on Consumer Lens, a product which involved analyzing survey data focused on current and developing consumer trends/patterns to identify popular choices amongst people across demographics such as age, employment, financial status etc. for the purpose of marketing.
    Currently, as a Data Engineer at Octro Inc., I develop data pipelines across multiple databases, develop table schemas; irrigate the data, and write Python APIs for creating subsets of Consumer Data using Octro’s gaming applications. Working through the process of data collection, storage, massage, and it’s usage for insight derivation geared towards business solutions, I have come to admire the versatility and power of analytics.
    Almost every industry has witnessed digitization over the past decade leading to astronomical growth in consumer and product data. This has created a demand for professionals who can provide tangible insights geared towards product improvement and business growth through the application of analytics and machine learning, which is something that intrigues me. To further my knowledge and move closer towards my goal of being a Data Scientist, I believe a full time academic program is a definite next step.
    The U Cincinnati’s MS in Information Systems Program through its courses including IS7036 Data Mining for Business Intelligence, IS7034 Data Warehousing and Business Intelligence, IS7032 Database Design, IS7011 Information and Technology Management, IS7020 Process Modeling etc. would give me the knowledge and expertise to not only break down complex data challenges but also work towards data management best practices and assisting strategic decision making. The valuable opportunities the program extends through experiential learning avenues combined with the option of pursuing a specialized graduate certificate in Data Science, I strongly believe that the Lindner College of Business’ distinguished faculty, flexible course structure and like-minded peers is the appropriate destination to broaden my perspectives and prepare me for the analytics requirement of the future.
